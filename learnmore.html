Mahathma Ghandi

Full name : Mohandas Karamchad Gandhi
Birth : 2nd October 1869
Died : 30 January 1948 (78 yrs)
Cause of Death : Assassination (gunshot wounds)
Citizenship : India
Spouse : Kasturba Gandhi
Children : Harilal / Manilal / Ramdas / Devdas
Mother : Putlibai Gandhi
Father : Karamchand Gandhi
Occupation : Lawyer / Politician / Activist / Writer.

Mahathma Ghandi is a greatest leader of the independence movement in India. He didn't care about the race, creed, caste or class and valued non-violence. Mahathma Ghandi was the person who led the freedom struggle to a non-violence movement. He left for england for higher education. Mahathma Ghandi became a lawyer and served in South Africa, he returned to his motherland in 1915.

During the first period he came to India, he was solving the problems among farmer communities and labourers. The struggle he carried out to solve the problems of the farmers was called as Champaran Peasant Movement. 

Holding sathyagara campaigns, non-cooperation with the government, civil disobedience
campaign and organizing street marches were the non violent strategies adopted
by Mahathma Ghandi. Mahathma Ghandi was the leader who turned India's Independence Movement into a struggle of the people. He believed that all the Indians should have the same equal rights. He was a very humble person.

He said that the indians should value the should value the products of their own country and he gave an example to all the Indians by wearing clothes made by himself using the cotton material woven by himself! He believed that such factors would contribute a lot towards the development of this country.

He acted as the leader of the independence movement of india to free the country from the rule of the British and became a leader who was loved and repected by all the Indians alike.

He was busy when he was in India, how ever he stepped into Sri Lanka in 1927. He stayed in this country for a period of about three weeks. During his stay, he traveled to many areas such as Colombo, Kalutara, Galle, Kandy and Jaffna, and adressed people. Where ever he travelled, he was warmly welcomed by the people in our country (SriLankans)
